Introduction¶

This project analyzes graduate employment outcomes across Singapore's polytechnics and universities. By examining datasets on employment rates, salaries, and graduate numbers, we aim to uncover trends and insights that can inform educational and career decisions.

Objectives¶

  • Compare employment rates and median salaries between university and polytechnic graduates.
  • Analyze trends in graduate numbers across different institutions and courses.
  • Identify factors influencing employment outcomes for graduates in Singapore.

Key Questions¶

  1. How do employment rates differ between university and polytechnic graduates over the years (2021–2023)?
  2. How do full-time permanent employment rates compare between fresh and Post-NS polytechnic graduates?
  3. Which courses or fields of study lead to the highest employment and salary outcomes?
  4. How have enrolment, graduation, and employment numbers changed across institutions and courses over time?

import csv
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

# Function to read CSV
def read_csv(filename):
    data = []
    try:
        with open(filename, mode='r', encoding='utf-8-sig') as file:
            reader = csv.DictReader(file)
            for row in reader:
                data.append(row)
    except FileNotFoundError:
        print(f"File not found: {filename}")
    return data

# Read datasets
poly_data = read_csv('IntakeEnrolmentandGraduatesofPolytechnicsbyCourse.csv')

# Aggregate graduate numbers by course
poly_grads = {}

for row in poly_data:
    course = row['course']
    graduates = row['graduates'].strip()
    try:
        graduates = int(graduates) if graduates else 0
    except:
        graduates = 0

    poly_grads[course] = poly_grads.get(course, 0) + graduates

# Get top 5 courses by graduate numbers
top_poly = sorted(poly_grads.items(), key=lambda x: x[1], reverse=True)[:5]

# Plotting
plt.figure(figsize=(10,6))

# Polytechnics
courses_poly = [x[0] for x in top_poly]
grads_poly = [x[1] for x in top_poly]
plt.bar(courses_poly, grads_poly, alpha=0.7, color='skyblue')

plt.title('Top 5 Polytechnic Courses by Graduate Numbers')
plt.xlabel('Course')
plt.ylabel('Number of Graduates')
plt.xticks(rotation=45, ha='right')
plt.tight_layout()
plt.grid(True)
plt.show()

Final Conclusion¶

This comprehensive analysis of graduate employment data in Singapore from 2021 to 2023 provides critical insights for students, educators, and policymakers.

  1. University vs Polytechnic Graduates: University graduates enjoy higher employment rates consistently across the years, highlighting the premium placed on degrees. This supports Objective 1 and answers Key Question 1 by confirming the gap in outcomes between different educational paths.

  2. Fresh vs Post-NS Polytechnic Graduates: Post-NS graduates demonstrate a clear advantage in securing full-time permanent jobs, revealing the importance of work readiness and experience. This addresses Objective 3 and Key Question 2, showing that employment outcomes are shaped by more than academic credentials.

  3. Course-Based Outcomes: Fields like Computing and Engineering provide the best employment and salary outcomes, indicating strong demand in these sectors. This supports Objective 3 and answers Key Question 3, guiding students on strategic course selection.

  4. Graduate Trends Over Time: All institutions show growth in graduate numbers, particularly universities. This observation ties to Objective 2 and Key Question 4, confirming a broader trend toward expanded access and completion in higher education.

In summary, the findings show Singapore’s education system is aligned with labor market demands but highlight areas for continued improvement, such as enhancing polytechnic employability and guiding students toward in-demand fields.
